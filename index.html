Our Story

A Church Built on
Faith & Community

Established in 2001, Landmark Baptist Church has been a beacon of hope and a gathering place for families across the Grand Valley. Nestled in the breathtaking landscape of Grand Junction, Colorado, we are a church that believes the Bible — every word of it — and loves every person who walks through our doors.

Our mission is simple: Love. Share. Reach. We love God, we share His Word, and we reach our community with the life-changing message of the Gospel. Whether you've been in church your whole life or you're walking in for the very first time, you belong here.

Statement of Faith

What We Believe

At Landmark Baptist Church, we believe the Bible — the King James Bible — to be the inspired, infallible, inerrant Word of God. Every sermon, every class, every conversation is anchored in its unchanging truth.

Our Statement of Faith is not exhaustive, but it declares the essentials of Bible-believing Christianity that guide everything we do.

Ask a Question

The Scriptures of the Old and New Testament were given by inspiration of God and are the only infallible, inerrant rule of faith and practice. We believe the King James Bible to be God's preserved Word for English-speaking people, authoritative in every matter it addresses.

Matthew 4:4Psalm 119:89II Timothy 3:16–17

There is one living and true God, revealed as Creator, Preserver, and Righteous Governor of the universe. The Father, the Son, and the Holy Ghost make up the Godhead — three Persons, yet one God, infinitely wise and good, alone worthy of our supreme love and worship.

Deuteronomy 6:4I John 5:7Matthew 28:19

Jesus Christ is God manifest in the flesh — in His divine nature, truly God; in His human nature, truly man; 100% both. The virgin-born, sinless, eternally begotten Son of God. Once crucified, He is now risen and glorified — our ever-present Saviour and Lord.

John 1:1–4, 14Philippians 2:5–11John 3:16

The Holy Ghost possesses all the attributes of God. He is personal and indwells every believer — serving as Reprover, Comforter, Guide, Sanctifier, and the Seal of our Redemption. He convicts the sinner and regenerates the believer.

John 16:7–14John 14:16–17Ephesians 1:13–14

Man was created innocent but fell into sin. Salvation is a free gift offered by God to man, wholly based on grace through personal faith in Jesus Christ — a genuine "born-again" experience. Not by works, not by baptism, not by church attendance — but by trusting Christ alone as personal Saviour.

Romans 6:23Ephesians 2:8–9Romans 10:9

All believers in Christ are saved by grace and kept by the power of God. Heaven is a definite, indescribably beautiful place reserved for the saved. Hell is a terrible, eternal reality for all who die in their sin. Baptism is the immersion of believers in water, representing the death, burial, and resurrection of Jesus Christ — an outward symbol of inward transformation, following conversion.

John 14:1–3Revelation 20:11–15Matthew 28:19
