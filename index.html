Our Story

A Church Built on the Word

For decades, Landmark Baptist Church has stood as a beacon of biblical truth in the heart of the Grand Valley. We are a congregation committed to expository preaching, genuine fellowship, and reaching our community with the life-changing message of the Gospel.

Whether you are a long-time believer or exploring faith for the first time, you will find a warm, welcoming family here — a place to belong, to grow, and to serve.

First Time Here?

What to Expect

We want your first visit to feel comfortable and welcoming. Here are a few things to know.

What should I wear?

Come as you are. Some dress up, some dress casually — what matters is that you're here. We want you to feel at home from the moment you walk in.

What about my kids?

We have safe, engaging programs for every age — nursery through high school. Your children will be in a secure, caring environment while you enjoy the service.

What will I hear?

Expository preaching straight from the King James Bible, combined with heartfelt congregational singing. Our services are Christ-centered and practical.

What We Believe

We believe the Bible to be the verbally inspired, inerrant Word of God and the only sufficient, certain, and authoritative rule of all saving knowledge, faith, and obedience. The King James Version is our text of choice.

We believe in one God, eternally existing in three Persons — Father, Son, and Holy Spirit — co-equal in nature, attributes, and perfection. Each Person is distinct, yet the Godhead is one.

We believe in the virgin birth, sinless life, substitutionary death, bodily resurrection, and glorious ascension of the Lord Jesus Christ. He is the only mediator between God and man.

Salvation is entirely by grace through faith in the finished work of Christ — not by works, merit, or ritual. Eternal security is the promise of every true believer.

We practice believer's baptism by immersion as a public declaration of faith, and observe the Lord's Supper as a memorial of Christ's sacrifice. Both are ordinances of the local church, not means of grace.
