Our Story

Established in Faith

Established in May 2001 with just 20 people, God has blessed Landmark Baptist Church with tremendous growth, leading us to our beautiful 30,000-square-foot campus on Ute Avenue.

What began in a small room without chairs, hymnals, or offering plates has flourished into a thriving community of faith. Over the years, we've witnessed countless souls saved, baptized, and added to our church family.

Frequently Asked Questions

How should I dress?

There is no dress code at Landmark Baptist Church. Come as you are! While many dress in traditional Sunday attire, our main goal is that you feel welcome and comfortable.

Am I expected to give?

No pressure to give — let the service be our gift to you. We hope you'll find a warm family spirit, truth from God's Word, and a place to grow in grace.

What about my children?

We have safe, clean nurseries and dynamic classes for all ages. Our greeters will gladly help direct you to age-appropriate rooms and services.

Our Doctrine

The Scriptures of the Old and New Testament were given by inspiration of God and are the only infallible, inerrant rule of faith and practice. We believe the King James Bible to be authoritative in every matter it addresses.

There is one living and true God, revealed in nature as the Creator, Preserver, and Righteous Governor of the universe. The Father, the Son, and the Holy Ghost make up the Godhead, yet as one God infinitely wise and good.

Jesus Christ is God manifest in the flesh; in His divine nature truly God; in His human nature, truly man. The mediator between God and man, once crucified, He is now risen and glorified and is our ever present Saviour and Lord.

The Scriptures assign to the Holy Ghost all the attributes of God. He is personal and indwells every believer. He is the Reprover, Comforter, Guide, Sanctifier, and the Seal of our Redemption.

Salvation is by grace through faith in the finished work of Christ. We believe in the eternal security of the believer — once saved, always saved — grounded in the unchanging promise of God's Word.

We believe in the bodily resurrection of the just and unjust — the saved to everlasting life and the unsaved to conscious, eternal separation from God. Heaven and Hell are literal, eternal places.
